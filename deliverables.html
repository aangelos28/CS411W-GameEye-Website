Risk Matrix

Technical Risks:
  • T1: Time constraints to learning new APIs.

  • T2: Video game database connectivity failure.

  • T3: Platform becomes so popular that the servers get flooded.

  • T4: Main database fails.

  • T5: The structure of source websites changes, causing web scraping to fail.
Customer Risks:
  • C1: Gamers may believe that they do not need GameEye in order to follow news of their favorite games, as they may follow their favorite games on social media or can look up any game they want on Google to get news.

  • C2: Those that run video game news websites may believe that GameEye is trying to do their job for them.

  • C3: Data breach exposed user information (in our case, emails and password hashes).

  • C4: User does not know how to use the application.
Technical Risk Mitigations:
  • T1: Independent skills, training and practice

  • T2: No mitigation; beyond control

  • T3: Make solution scalable; use load balancers, have multiple backend servers and multiple database servers

  • T4: Have more instances of the main database

  • T5: Scrape RSS feeds, which are far less likely to change
Customer Risk Mitigations:
  • C1: GameEye provides features that its competition may lack, such as noise filtering and the ability to track games.

  • C2: GameEye is not meant to be a replacement for video game news websites, as such, it provides links to already existing news sources which increases traffic to them.

  • C3: Store less sensitive info; use a 3rd party authentication provider; do penetration testing; use database encryption

  • C4: Provide instructional material (in-app tutorial, videos, etc.)
